The 90/180 problem, explained

The Schengen Area lets you stay 90 days within any 180-day rolling window. Sounds simple. It is not.

  • It is not 90 days per calendar year. The 180-day window rolls forward every single day, recalculating what counts.
  • Multiple short trips make manual counting nearly impossible. A week in Spain, two in Portugal, a weekend in France — it adds up fast.
  • Overstaying can mean fines, entry bans, or deportation. Border agents will check your stamps.
  • Nomad Visa Tracker does the math for you. Enter your trips, and the rolling window calculation happens automatically. Always accurate, always up to date.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Schengen 90/180 rule actually work?
The Schengen Area allows non-EU citizens to stay up to 90 days within any rolling 180-day period. The key word is "rolling" — the 180-day window shifts forward every day, and each day the system checks backward 180 days to count how many of those you spent in Schengen territory. Nomad Visa Tracker handles this calculation automatically as you log your trips.
Does the app track US visa limits too?
Yes. Beyond Schengen, the app tracks US B1/B2 visa and ESTA limitations. It monitors your cumulative days of stay and can alert you before you approach limits that could affect future visa applications or trigger the IRS substantial presence test.
What are the tax residency warnings?
Many countries consider you a tax resident if you spend more than 183 days per year in their territory. Nomad Visa Tracker monitors your days per country and warns you well before you hit these thresholds, giving you time to plan your next move.
Does it work offline?
Absolutely. The app is built offline-first. All your data is stored locally on your device. No internet connection is needed to log trips, check your remaining days, or receive alerts. This is critical when you are at a border crossing with no signal.
